Who you'd be talking to

I worked directly with John Hirst CBE during his time as Chief Executive, ran cross-governmental communications campaigns spanning the Met Office, DEFRA, the Department of Energy and Climate Change and the Department for Business, Innovation and Skills, and stood in front of MPs in Whitehall to talk about innovation in the public sector. How it works

First we talk. Then we play.

It only works with the person who knows the business best, the founder, or the expert at its centre. Conviction in the room is the one thing I need. I bring everything else.

Day one

A day with your expert

We talk, and we genuinely don't know where it will go. I share what's working in other sectors and at the edge of technology. For the first half, they defend why things are done the way they are. And they should.

The shift

The interesting middle

By the end of the day we've found it. They've moved from where they started. I've moved from "anything is possible" toward something confined, structured and real. Neither of us reaches that point alone.

The decision

I present what we found to your board of directors. Then, together, you decide how to proceed. No assumptions, no lock-in, just a clear view of a future worth building.
